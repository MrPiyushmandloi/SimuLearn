Physics Tooltips

This simulation demonstrates the conversion between **Potential Energy (PE)** and **Kinetic Energy (KE)** as a ball falls and bounces.

Potential Energy (PE)

Stored energy due to position. It is **maximum** at the highest point and **zero** at the ground (our reference point).

Kinetic Energy (KE)

Energy of motion. It is **maximum** just before hitting the ground and **zero** at the highest points where the ball momentarily stops.

Total Energy

The sum of KE and PE. In a real-world bounce, the total energy **decreases** slightly due to energy loss (sound, heat) represented by the **Bounce Coefficient**.
