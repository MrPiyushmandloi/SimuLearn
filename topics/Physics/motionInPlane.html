üõ´ What is Motion in a Plane?

Motion in a plane is 2D motion where object moves in X-Y plane (not straight line, not 3D space). Examples: Projectile motion, circular motion, boat crossing river [web:1][web:2]
  • Needs two coordinates (x, y) to describe position
  • Combines horizontal + vertical motion simultaneously
  • Key topics: Vectors, Projectile motion, Relative motion [web:1]

üìè Scalars - Complete Explanation

Scalars have only magnitude, no direction. Follow simple algebra rules.
Scalar Operations:
Addition: \( s = s_1 + s_2 \) [web:1]
Subtraction: \( s = s_1 - s_2 \) [web:1]
Multiplication: \( s = k \times s_1 \) (k = scalar) [web:1]
  • Examples: Mass (5kg), Speed (20m/s), Distance (10m), Time (5s), Temperature (25¬∞C)
  • Key Point: 10m North + 10m South = 0m (NOT vectors!)

‚û°Ô∏è Vectors - Complete Explanation

Vectors have magnitude AND direction. Represented by arrows (length = magnitude, arrow = direction).

Vector Notation: \(\vec{A} = 5\hat{i} + 3\hat{j}\) [web:1]

Magnitude: \( |\vec{A}| = \sqrt{A_x^2 + A_y^2} \) [web:1]

Vector Addition (Triangle Law):
\( \vec{R} = \vec{A} + \vec{B} \) [web:1]
\( R = \sqrt{A^2 + B^2 + 2AB\cos\theta} \) [web:1]
Vector Subtraction: \( \vec{A} - \vec{B} = \vec{A} + (-\vec{B}) \) [web:1]

üìê Projectile Motion - All Formulas

1. Components of Initial Velocity:
\( v_{0x} = v_0 \cos\theta \), \( v_{0y} = v_0 \sin\theta \) [web:1]

2. Position Equations:
\( x = (v_0 \cos\theta) t \) [web:1]
\( y = (v_0 \sin\theta) t - \frac{1}{2} g t^2 \) [web:1]

3. Key Results:
Time of Flight: \( T = \frac{2 v_0 \sin\theta}{g} \) [web:1]
Maximum Range: \( R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin 2\theta}{g} \) (Max at Œ∏ = 45¬∞) [web:1]
Maximum Height: \( H = \frac{v_0^2 \sin^2\theta}{2g} \) [web:1]

Trajectory is always parabola: \( y = x\tan\theta - \frac{gx^2}{2v_0^2\cos^2\theta} \)[web:1]
